Big Data: A collection of very large and complex data packages that are difficult to handle with traditional database management systems (DBMS) in terms of storage, search, representation, and analysis. The term Big Data can be explained as a multi-type data source with sizes of hundreds of terabytes, even petabytes, or even more per packet of data! Big data is of high importance, it provides a high competitive advantage for companies if they can benefit from them and process them because they provide a deeper understanding of their customers and their requirements and this helps to take appropriate and appropriate decisions within the company in a more effective way, based on information extracted from customer databases and thus increase efficiency and profit and reduce and Losses. Organized: structured data is often stored in a database or other data management platform, and can be easily accessed and processed to provide arranged output. Disorganized: Usually the largest data sets - most big data is disorganized, which means it cannot be organized or categorized easily. Semi-structured: As the name implies, semi-structured data is not organized in nature at first, but when it is analyzed or absorbed, it can begin to take a more structured form.
